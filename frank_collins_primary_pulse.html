Launch of School of Medicine Primary Care Pathway Program

We are pleased to announce the launch of the Measey Primary Care Pathway Program at the Perelman School of Medicine, generously supported by the Benjamin and Mary Siddons Measey Foundation. Penn Medicine’s Shaping the Future of Medicine 2.0 Strategic Plan specifically recommended the development of a system-wide educational model to train the next generation of primary care providers in Penn Medicine. Beginning July 2019, the Measey Primary Care Pathway Program will prepare the next generation of medical students interested in general internal medicine, family medicine, pediatrics and, geriatrics to lead the transformation of patient-centered, high-value primary care delivery.

Penn Integrated Care Team Recognized at the UPHS CEQI Awards

The Primary Care Service Line’s Penn Integrated Care team was recognized at the UPHS CEQI awards, winning the 2018 UPHS Operational Award for the implementation of Penn Integrated Care (Collaborative Care Behavioral Health program). Since the program launched in early 2018, thousands of patients have been connected to mental health care, either within the participating primary care practices or in the community. Thank you to all the individuals in Primary Care, Psychiatry, Finance and IS who helped make this possible, and we look forward to continued growth and success of the program.

LGH Scribe Program Success

Penn The LGH Physician group contracted with ScribeAmerica in the Fall of 2017 to implement in-person scribe services with a goal to reduce provider burnout and improve satisfaction. A one-year pilot was conducted with ten physicians and one advanced practice clinician, resulting in significant improvement in provider well-being. Providers saw increased coding accuracy, greater face time with patients and reduced time spent after hours. With the success of the pilot, LGH Physician leadership has approved both continuation and scaling of the program. Our other Primary Care entities will look to LGH for lessons learned in this effort.

Clinical Decision Support to Reduce High-Risk Medication Orders

In February 2019, Penn Medicine implemented an initiative to reduce high-risk medications prescribed to patients over 65. Primary Care providers will now be alerted when ordering high-risk medications. This clinical decision support can facilitate the selection of alternative medicines and reduce common adverse drug events, like falls and delirium. Practice Leads and Practice Managers can now view the most frequently prescribed high-risk medications at their practices in PCSL Tableau High-Risk Medications Dashboard (across the PCSL, the five most common high-risk medications include promethazine, diphenhydramine, cyclobenzaprine, amitriptyline, and zolpidem).

March is Colon Cancer Awareness Month

Each year, 140,000 Americans are diagnosed with colon cancer. If caught early, there is a 90 percent chance it can be cured. A simple colonoscopy can make a world of difference. In an effort to spread awareness, an email is being sent to all qualifying Penn Medicine Primary Care patients to encourage them to schedule a colonoscopy by calling 215-615-2580 and download a free prevention guide.

Practice Spotlight – Penn Medical Associates of Cherry Hill: Colorectal Cancer Screening

Accurately capturing patients’ up-to-date colorectal cancer screening information can be challenging. At Penn Medical Associates of Cherry Hill, 70% of eligible patients have up-to-date colorectal cancer screening results on file. Medical Assistants (MAs) at this practice are credited for this accomplishment, pending appropriate tests and editing modifiers. The Cherry Hill MAs are also responsible for obtaining colorectal screening reports, searching for reports that may not have pulled into Health Maintenance and working with Medical Records to have them scanned correctly.
